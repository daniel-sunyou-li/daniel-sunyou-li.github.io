Daniel Sunyou Li (Ph.D.)

My name is Daniel Li, and I am currently a postdoctoral research in the physics department at Northwestern University working under Professor Mayda Velasco. I am a member of the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) collaboration, which is one of the experimental collaboration associated with the European Center for Nuclear Physics (CERN) in Geneva, Switzerland. My current work focuses on searching for the rare Standard Model decay of the Higgs boson to a Z boson and photon, which has yet to be discovered, as well as developing a new sub-detector system called the High-Granularity Calorimeter to be implemented in the upgraded CMS detector as part of the High-Luminosity Large Hadron Collider in 2030.

I joined the CMS collaboration in 2018 as a doctoral student under Professors Meenakshi Narain and Ulrich Heintz at Brown University. I earned my doctorate in experimental particle physics in 2024 and my dissertation topic was the first search for triple-top quark production at the LHC. Before joining Brown University, I was an undergraduate at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where I majored in Engineering Physics and minored in Electrical Engineering, Applied Mathematics, Statistics and Arabic Language and Studies. While at UIUC, I worked under Professor Matthias Perdekamp for two years on the Zero-Degree Calorimeter project for the ATLAS collider--the sister experiment and collaboration to the CMS experiment.

Outside of academic physics research, I am interested in pedagogical methods and the history of physics. During my doctoral program, I was regularly involved with Brown University's Sheridan Center for Teaching and Learning, as well as the Summer at Brown pre-college program as an instructor. One of my proudest achievements while at Brown was earning the Archambault Teaching Award in 2022 for my pre-college course titled, "What Does It Take to Discover a Particle?"
